Correct Peptide Reconstitution

Our compounds are delivered in a lyophilized (freeze-dried) state to preserve stability and purity. Before use in research settings, they must be reconstituted with a suitable solvent. Proper technique ensures consistency, accuracy, and reliable outcomes.

Begin in a sterile environment. Use gloves, alcohol swabs, and clean surfaces. Ensure all materials are prepared before starting the reconstitution process.

Select bacteriostatic water as your solvent. Allow both peptide and solvent to reach room temperature before use to ensure proper dissolution.

Slowly introduce the solvent into the vial. Tilt at a slight angle and avoid direct pressure on the powder. Do not shake—allow it to dissolve naturally.

Store reconstituted solutions in refrigeration (2–8°C). For best results, use within several weeks to maintain integrity and effectiveness.
